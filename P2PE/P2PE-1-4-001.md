# P2PE-1-4-001 — Section 4 N/A table: applicability to sub-requirements and mixed-status requirements in the P2PE P-ROV report

## Situation

The PCI P2PE KMS, EMS, DMS P-ROV report template includes the following instruction in Section 4 (Findings and Observations):

> "Every requirement denoted as 'N/A' in the reporting section below must be documented in this table and vice versa."

In practice, P-ROV reports regularly contain requirements where:

- The overall (parent) requirement is **In Place**, but one or more sub-requirements are **Not Applicable**
- A sub-requirement contains both In Place and Not Applicable elements within the same row (a "mixed-status" sub-requirement)

Without a firm agreed position, assessors have been handling these cases inconsistently: some include sub-requirement N/A entries in Section 4, some do not. This creates reviewable inconsistency across Patronusec P-ROV deliverables and risks attracting a finding during PCI SSC quality review.

**Concrete examples:**

**Example A — Requirement 1-4:**
| Requirement | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| 1-4 (overall) | In Place |
| 1-4.a | In Place |
| 1-4.b | Not Applicable — organisation uses a PCI PTS-approved device rather than a FIPS-approved HSM |

**Example B — Requirement 6-4.b:**
| Requirement | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| 6-4.b | Mixed — one element In Place, one element Not Applicable |

---

## Task

Determine:

1. Does the Section 4 N/A instruction apply only to top-level (overall) requirements marked N/A, or also to individual sub-requirements marked N/A?
2. For a sub-requirement that is partially In Place and partially Not Applicable, does it require an entry in Section 4, and if so, what should that entry contain?
3. What is the standard format for mixed-status entries in Section 4?

---

## Action

The team reviewed the Section 4 instruction text and considered the intent of the N/A documentation table.

**On the scope of the instruction:**

The instruction states "every requirement denoted as N/A" without limiting this to top-level requirements. The team agreed that reading the instruction narrowly (top-level only) would create an incomplete audit record — a reviewer examining Section 4 would be unaware that sub-requirements had been marked N/A and would have no documented justification for those N/A markings. This would undermine the purpose of the table.

The team agreed the instruction applies to **all requirements and sub-requirements** marked N/A, regardless of whether the parent requirement is In Place.

**On mixed-status sub-requirements:**

Where a sub-requirement row contains both In Place and Not Applicable elements (as in Example B), the N/A element must still be addressed in Section 4. The entry should:
- Identify the specific clause or element that is N/A (not the entire sub-requirement)
- Provide a clear justification for why that element is not applicable
- Be specific enough that a reviewer can cross-reference it to the relevant part of the reporting section without ambiguity

**On format:**

For a full N/A sub-requirement (Example A):

> Section 4 entry: **Requirement 1-4.b** — Not Applicable.
> *Justification:* The organisation uses a PCI PTS-approved device rather than a FIPS-approved HSM. The FIPS HSM option specified in this sub-requirement is therefore not applicable to this implementation.

For a partial N/A sub-requirement (Example B style):

> Section 4 entry: **Requirement 6-4.b (partial)** — the [specific clause, e.g. "key custodian quorum requirement"] element is Not Applicable.
> *Justification:* [Reason why that specific element does not apply.]
> Note: The remaining elements of 6-4.b are In Place and are documented in the reporting section.

---

## Result

**Agreed position:** [To be confirmed at the council meeting — proposed position below for discussion]

*Proposed:* The Section 4 N/A table instruction applies to all requirements and sub-requirements marked N/A, including sub-requirements whose parent requirement is In Place. For mixed-status sub-requirements, a partial entry is required in Section 4, specifically identifying and justifying the N/A element. The entry format must be specific enough to cross-reference to the reporting section without ambiguity.

**Conditions:** This position applies to P-ROV reports using the KMS/EMS/DMS P-ROV report template. It should be reviewed if PCI SSC publishes a revised template with different Section 4 instructions.
